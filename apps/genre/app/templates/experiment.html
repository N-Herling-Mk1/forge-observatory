B.E.A.R. D.O.W.N.

Bayesian Evaluation of Audio Representations — A Deep Optimized Waveform Network
GOAL BEARDOWN was not chasing a leaderboard. It was a vehicle for two things: testing a multimodal architecture for music-genre recognition, and exploring epistemic / reliability metrics — model selection that rewards calibrated uncertainty and stability, not accuracy alone. The accuracy target (75–80% on GTZAN, via k=3 cross-validation and broad random hyperparameter sweeps) was the floor, not the point.

The architecture

A multimodal network in three stages: two deterministic encoders feed a fusion block, which feeds a probabilistic Bayesian head. Each 30-second clip enters as both a spectrogram image and a 58-dimensional feature vector, so the model sees timbre/time structure and engineered audio statistics at once.

Dual encoders → fusion → Bayesian head. Examples are sampled into minibatches (①); weights are sampled only inside the Bayesian head (②). All features enter every pass — no feature bagging.
1 · Dual encoders deterministic
A compact CNN (conv–ReLU–pool → flatten → dense) over the spectrogram, and a small MLP (ReLU + structured dropout) over the 58 tabular features. Each projects its modality into a latent embedding.
2 · Fusion block deterministic
Combines the two embeddings by concatenation or a gated mechanism (chosen per sampled hyperparameter) into one unified multimodal representation.
3 · Bayesian classification head probabilistic
Two variants: a Flipout dense layer (variational inference) or an MC-Dropout dense layer (repeated stochastic passes). Outputs a softmax over the 10 genres plus epistemic and aleatoric uncertainty, aggregated by Monte-Carlo evaluation.

Training objective

BEARDOWN trains as one model — one optimizer, one loss. The loss is the (negative) ELBO, the evidence lower bound. To settle the recurring question: the ELBO is the objective, not the optimizer. We maximize the ELBO, which is identical to minimizing its negative (below); the optimizer — Adam / AdamW / RMSprop, chosen per sweep — is the gradient-descent algorithm that does the minimizing.

$$\mathcal{L}(\theta,\phi)=\underbrace{\mathbb{E}_{q_\phi(w)}\!\big[-\log p(y\mid x,\,\theta_{\mathrm{cnn}},\theta_{\mathrm{mlp}},\theta_{\mathrm{fus}},\,w)\big]}_{\text{expected NLL — data fit}}\;+\;\frac{1}{N}\,\underbrace{\mathrm{KL}\!\big(q_\phi(w)\,\Vert\,p(w)\big)}_{\text{regularizer on head weights}}$$
Deterministic params θ_cnn · θ_mlp · θ_fus
The CNN, MLP, and fusion weights are point estimates. They sit inside the NLL term — they shape $x\rightarrow$ logits — and carry no KL term (equivalently, a Dirac prior with KL = 0). Trained by ordinary backprop.
Variational params q_φ(w)
Only the head's weights are distributions — a mean-field Gaussian $q_\phi(w)=\mathcal{N}(\mu,\sigma^2)$, sampled via Flipout using the reparameterization $w=\mu+\sigma\odot\epsilon,\ \epsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$ so gradients pass through the sample. This is the only block contributing a KL term.
One step, end to end
The loss is differentiable through the whole graph, so a single optimizer step updates $\theta$ and $(\mu,\sigma)$ together — gradients flow from the head back through fusion into both encoders. There is no "train the CNN, then the Bayesian part." Sweep knobs prior_scale, posterior_scale_init, and kl_factor rescale $p$, $q$, and the KL weight respectively.

Data & preprocessing

GTZAN's spectrograms and feature CSVs don't align perfectly — after merging the two modalities, ~10% of clips couldn't be matched and were dropped, leaving roughly 900 fully matched multimodal samples. Spectrograms were resized, pixel-normalized, and lightly augmented (time/frequency masking, amplitude jitter); tabular features were z-score standardized with winsorization of spectral-flux and energy outliers (no imputation — unmatched rows were removed during alignment). This dashboard's EDA is the integrity layer underneath that step.

Two questions

Q1
Baseline performance
Can the hybrid Bayesian / non-Bayesian model reach 75–80% accuracy? Measured with classical metrics — precision, recall, F1, per-genre breakdowns, ROC curves, confusion matrices — alongside Bayesian diagnostics.
Q2
Reliability-aware evaluation central contribution
The Robust Ranking Metric (RRM) folds accuracy, cross-validation stability, and posterior dispersion into a single reliability-aware score. It correlates only moderately with accuracy (r ≈ 0.62) and weakly with off-diagonal confusion noise (r ≈ 0.26) — i.e. it captures reliability dimensions that accuracy alone misses. Paired with rank aggregation and a Pareto front over the accuracy-vs-reliability tradeoff.
